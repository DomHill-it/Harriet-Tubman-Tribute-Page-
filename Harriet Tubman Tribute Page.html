Harriet Tubman

More than just a conductor for the Underground Railroad

Here's a timeline of Harriet Tubmans Life

  • 1820 - Born on a plantation in Dorchester County Maryland
  • 1832 - At Age 12 Harriet intervened in fight between an enslaved person and the overseer and was struck with a heavy weight
  • 1840 - Harriets father was set free.
  • 1844 - Married John Tubman. Changed her last name from Ross to Tubman.
  • 1849 - Led herself, her brothers (Ben and Henry) and others escape thier Maryland Plantation. She and the others traveled 90 miles North to Pennsylvania with the help of the Underground Railroad
  • 1850 - Harriet led people North to Canada to escape the fugitive and freed workers that were free after the release of the Fugitive Slave Act
  • 1861 - Recuited to assist fugitive eslaved people at Fort Monroe and worked as a nurse, cook, and laundress.
  • 1863 - Became the head of espionage & scout network for the Union Army.
  • 1869 - Marries former ensalved man and Civil War veteran Nelson Davis.
  • 1896 - Opens the Harriet Tubman Home for Aged and Indigent Colored People.
  • 1913 - Dies from pneumonia.
  • 2016 - United States Treasury announced that Harriets image will replace former President and slaveowner Andrew Jackson on the twenty dollar bill.
